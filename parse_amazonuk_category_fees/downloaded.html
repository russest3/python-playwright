Referral fees

For every item sold, selling partners pay Amazon a percentage of the total price—including item price, shipping cost, and any gift-wrapping charges—or a minimum amount, whichever is greater. Referral fees are in addition to selling plan fees.

Other costs

When calculating the full cost of selling in Amazon stores, be sure to consider additional selling expenses you may incur, and other optional programmes designed to help you increase sales

Additional selling fees

Inventory fees
If you store inventory in an Amazon fulfilment centre—available to selling partners who use FBA or Multi-Channel Fulfilment—in addition to monthly inventory storage fees, you may incur other fees, such long-term storage fees, overage fees, and removal order fees.
Learn more about inventory-related fees on Seller Central Help
High-volume listing fees
The High Volume Listing Fee will only apply if you exceed 2 million SKUs in a given month, and it will be applied to your highest number of SKUs above 2 million at any time during that month. For each active non-media SKU over 2 million, a fee of £0.0003 per SKU will apply.
Learn more about high-volume listing fees
Refund administration fee
If you refund a customer for an order for which you have already received payment, Amazon will refund you the amount of the referral fee you paid for the item(s), minus the applicable Refund Administration Fee, which is the lesser of £5.00 or 20% of the applicable referral fee.

For example, if you refund a customer the GBP 10.00 total sales price of an item in a category with a 15% referral fee, your Refund Administration Fee will be £0.30 (£10.00 x 15% referral fee = £1.50).

Frequently Asked Questions

Get answers to frequently asked questions about using Amazon for eCommerce.
How are Amazon FBA fees calculated?
FBA fees or fulfilment fees are calculated per item depending on its category, size and weight, and the channel on which the item is being sold. These cover the costs of storing your items in Amazon fulfilment centres, picking, packaging and shipping them once they are bought, and providing customer care.

These fees are charged per calendar month for all items stored in Amazon fulfilment centres. Based on the fulfilment channel used, you can get a preview of your revenue estimates (both total sales proceeds as well as per-unit sold estimates) using the FBA revenue calculator for specific items listed on Amazon.
What is the Amazon FBA Calculator?
The FBA calculator is a preview tool that you can use to check and compare the estimated revenue for all the available fulfilment options for the products you have listed on Amazon and registered for Fulfilment by Amazon (FBA). You can enter your total expected sales volumes to estimate your total proceeds, check per-unit sold estimates, and also compare the fees and profitability between different fulfilment methods.
What is the difference between Amazon Seller fees and FBA fees?
Amazon fees refers to the total charges all sellers have to pay in order to have products listed and sold on the Amazon website. This includes the referral fee, which is a percentage of the total price of your product excluding any taxes. It is charged for each item sold and varies by category.

Then, based on your selling plan, you may incur a per-item fee or a monthly fee. For example, the Professional Selling Plan requires a monthly subscription fee that also affords the user a range of benefits.

Apart from this, certain sellers may also need to pay additional fees either for premium services or marketing or for other reasons. You can refer to this page for more comprehensive information.

FBA (Fulfilment By Amazon) Fees, on the other hand, are only charged for products that you have listed under this particular service. It is charged per unit based on the item's category, size and weight, and covers the cost to store, pick, pack and ship your products using FBA. If you have opted for this, these fees will be charged along with the other Amazon fees mentioned above.
How much does Amazon charge for storage of products?
Sellers have to pay a monthly inventory storage charge (storage fees), for the space occupied by all items stored in Amazon Fulfilment centres. This is calculated based on the daily average volume of the inventory, which is measured in cubic feet in the UK, and cubic metres in Europe.

The fee per cubic foot/metre varies based on the calendar month - it is lower from January to September, and a slightly higher amount is charged for the year-end, high-traffic months from October to December. The exact amount also depends on the category your product falls in, as well as its dimensions (size).

There is a separate fee structure for the storage of items that are classified as hazmat or dangerous goods.
What pricing plans does Amazon offer for sellers?
Amazon provides two pricing plans for sellers: the Individual and the Professional plans.

The Individual plan charges £0.75 (excluding VAT) per unit sold, while the Professional plan costs £25 (excluding VAT) per month, regardless of the number of units sold. Please note that additional fees may apply, including shipping, gift-wrapping, or a minimum fee, depending on which is greater. Referral fees are separate from the selling plan fees.
Do Amazon Seller pricing plans include a monthly fee option?
Yes, Amazon Seller pricing plans include a monthly fee option. The Professional plan costs £25 (excluding VAT) per month, no matter how many units you sell. This plan offers more features and flexibility compared to the Individual plan.
What is the referral fee, and how is it determined for Amazon sellers?
A referral fee is a commission that Amazon charges sellers for each product sold through its website. This fee is a percentage of the total sales price, which includes the item price and any additional charges, such as shipping or gift wrapping, paid by the buyer. The referral fee varies depending on the product category and also include a per-item minimum fee.

You can see more details here.
What is a closing fee, and in what situations does it apply to Amazon sellers?
A closing fee on Amazon refers to a fixed fee charged on certain types of product sales, typically in addition to other selling fees like the referral fee. This fee is most commonly applied to media items such as books, DVDs, music, and video games.
